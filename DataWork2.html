Chart:

This chart shows the number of inmates in the death row dataset by state. The bars are sorted from highest to lowest to make it easier to see which states have the largest death row populations.

What the data shows

The death row dataset shows that inmates are concentrated in a small number of states. A few states contain far more inmates than the rest, while many other states have much smaller death row populations. Specifically, the top few states have significantly higher numbers of inmates compared to the rest, which is evident from the steep drop in the height of the bars after the first few. This is likely due to their higher overall population sizes. The numbers drop quickly after the first few bars, showing that death row incarceration in this dataset is unevenly distributed across states. This could be due to some states abolishing the death penalty. Overall, the chart highlights how a limited number of states account for the largest share of inmates, however it's interesting to note that the dataset does not have NYC in the Data Set considering we only abolished it in June 2004 and we have similar population sizes compared to the other top states.
